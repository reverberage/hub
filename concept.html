The Asymmetry Problem

The tools used to produce disinformation have advanced by orders of magnitude in the last eighteen months. The tools used to verify information have not. This is not a technology problem — it is an infrastructure problem, and it has a name: the verification gap.
When a single operator with a consumer-grade LLM can generate five hundred convincing press releases in an afternoon, a newsroom staffed by fourteen people with Google search and reverse image lookup has already lost. The ratio is not 2:1 or 10:1. It is closer to 500:0 on any given Tuesday.
What the Reuters Institute survey makes clear is that editors understand this. They feel it every morning when they open their inboxes. What they lack is not awareness — it is leverage.
